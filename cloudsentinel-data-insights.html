The Problem

Are You Running Your Business Blind?

Most small businesses rely on memory and gut feeling. When you track and review your numbers, small decisions become smarter — and the difference compounds over time.

Know What's Actually Working

Track which products, services, or campaigns bring real revenue — so you invest in what grows, not what feels good.

Cut Costs Intelligently

Spot where money is leaking — overstocked items, slow-paying clients, or underused resources — before it becomes a crisis.

Prove Impact to Funders

NGOs and project teams with clean data reports attract more grants and donor trust than those relying on narrative alone.

Understand Your Customers

Simple purchase patterns reveal what customers want, when they buy, and why they leave — so you can serve them better.

The Process

What Data-Driven Decision Making Looks Like

You do not need a full analytics team. You need a simple, consistent system — and someone to set it up for you.

01

Collect

Capture sales, expenses, customer activity, and project outputs consistently — in a spreadsheet, app, or simple database.

02

Clean

Remove duplicates, fix errors, and standardise formats so your numbers actually reflect reality.

03

Analyse

Look for trends, peaks, gaps, and patterns. Even basic averages and totals reveal what is and is not working.

04

Visualise

Turn numbers into charts and dashboards so the whole team can understand the story at a glance.

05

Decide

Act on what the data shows — adjust pricing, restock products, reassign staff, or report to donors with confidence.

Practical Digital Tools That Do the Heavy Lifting

Dashboards

Data Collection

Business Intelligence

Power BI (Free tier)

Connect multiple data sources and build interactive reports. Ideal for growing businesses ready for the next step.

POS & Sales

Free or low-cost accounting tools that automatically generate reports — revenue, expenses, profit margins.

Automation

Zapier / Make (free tier)

Automatically move data between your tools so nothing is entered twice — saving hours every week.
