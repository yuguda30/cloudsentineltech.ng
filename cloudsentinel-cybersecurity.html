Why It Matters

Cybersecurity Is Not Just for Big Companies

SMEs, schools, and NGOs are frequent targets precisely because attackers know small organisations often have fewer protections. One breach can cost your business its reputation, its customers, and months of recovery time.

Financial Loss

A single phishing attack or fraudulent transfer can drain a business account. Recovery is slow and often incomplete.

Customer Trust

If client data is exposed, your reputation suffers. Customers may never return — and word spreads fast.

Compliance Risk

Nigerian data regulations and NGO grant requirements increasingly mandate basic security standards. Non-compliance risks penalties.

Threat Landscape

Common Digital Threats to Watch Out For

These are the most frequent attacks targeting businesses and organisations in Nigeria today.

High Risk

Phishing Emails & SMS

Fake messages that appear to be from banks, FIRS, or known contacts — designed to steal passwords and payment details.

High Risk

Ransomware

Malware that locks your files and demands payment. Small businesses are prime targets because they often lack backups.

Medium Risk

Social Engineering

Attackers impersonate suppliers, staff, or officials — using phone calls or WhatsApp to trick employees into transferring money.

Medium Risk

Weak Passwords & Accounts

Reusing simple passwords across business accounts is one of the easiest ways attackers gain access — often without you knowing.

Medium Risk

Unsecured Wi-Fi

Using public or open Wi-Fi for business transactions exposes login credentials and financial data to interception.

High Risk

Fake Apps & Websites

Counterfeit versions of banking or government portals harvest your credentials and payment info instantly.

Safety Habits

Simple Safety Habits That Make a Big Difference

You do not need expensive software to stay safer online. These habits cost nothing and stop most attacks.

  • 🔐
    Use strong, unique passwordsA different password for every account. Use a free password manager like Bitwarden if needed.
  • 📱
    Enable two-factor authenticationAdd a second step (SMS or app) to all important accounts — email, banking, social media.
  • 🔗
    Think before you clickHover over links to check the real URL. If something feels urgent or strange, verify by phone first.
  • 💾
    Back up your data regularlyStore copies on an external drive or cloud. A backup means ransomware cannot permanently destroy your business.
  • 🔄
    Keep software updatedUpdates patch security holes attackers actively exploit. Enable automatic updates whenever possible.
  • 🎓
    Train your teamOne employee clicking a bad link can expose everything. Brief monthly reminders save businesses.
